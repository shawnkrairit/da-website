Termination, Severance & Labour Court

Thai employment law is fundamentally protective of employees. The Labour Court demonstrates a consistent pro-employee orientation. We advise employers on lawful termination procedures, statutory severance calculation, and the grounds for termination without severance under Section 119 of the Labour Protection Act.

Statutory severance ranges from 30 days (120 days–1 year of service) to 400 days (over 20 years), with an additional special severance of 15 days per year for employees with 6+ years of service. We also represent employers before the Labour Court in wrongful termination and wage disputes.

What We Handle
Termination procedures and notices, statutory severance calculation, termination for cause (Section 119), Labour Court representation, wrongful termination defence, wage disputes, non-compete enforcement, redundancy and layoff programmes, settlement negotiations.

Workforce Restructuring

Business transfers, factory closures, and downsizing in Thailand require careful compliance with the Labour Protection Act. Section 75 requires 60 days' notice to the Labour Department for temporary work suspension. Business transfers under Section 13 impose automatic assumption of all obligations by the buyer.

We advise on structuring compliant redundancy programmes that minimise litigation risk while achieving the employer's commercial objectives — including mass termination, partial closure, outsourcing, and post-M&A integration.

What We Handle
Mass redundancy programmes, Section 75 notifications, business transfer compliance (Section 13), factory and operations closure, post-M&A workforce integration, outsourcing structuring, voluntary separation schemes, employment audit and gap remediation.

Data Privacy & PDPA

End-to-end PDPA compliance advisory — from gap analysis and programme design through DPO services, breach response, and cross-border transfer frameworks. Thailand's PDPA has been in full force since 1 June 2022, with enforcement actively increasing.

PDPA Compliance Programme Design

Thailand's PDPA imposes obligations on any organisation collecting or processing personal data of individuals in Thailand — regardless of where the organisation is established. Penalties reach THB 5 million in administrative fines, with criminal penalties of up to one year imprisonment and civil liability of up to 2× actual damages.

We provide gap analysis against PDPA requirements, design compliance roadmaps, draft privacy policies and Records of Processing Activities (ROPA), establish lawful basis frameworks, and implement consent management systems tailored to your operations.

What We Handle
PDPA gap analysis, compliance roadmap and programme design, privacy policies and notices, Records of Processing Activities (ROPA), lawful basis assessment, consent management, vendor and processor agreements, data subject rights procedures, Privacy Impact Assessments (DPIA), PDPA training and awareness.

Taxation

Strategic tax advisory for domestic and cross-border transactions — integrating the Thai Revenue Code with Thailand's 60+ double taxation agreements to optimise structures and manage risk. Significant changes to foreign-sourced income taxation took effect 1 January 2024.

Corporate Tax Planning & Compliance

Thailand imposes corporate income tax (CIT) at 20%, with reduced rates for SMEs and BOI-promoted companies receiving up to 13 years' full exemption. We advise on tax-efficient structuring of Thai operations, profit repatriation via dividends and intercompany charges, and permanent establishment risk management for foreign companies operating in Thailand.

Since 1 January 2024, Thai tax residents are assessable on foreign-sourced income remitted to Thailand regardless of when earned — reversing the previous year-of-income rule. We advise expatriates and companies on the implications of this change.

What We Handle
CIT planning and structuring, BOI tax incentives, PE risk management, profit repatriation, VAT compliance, specific business tax, withholding tax, stamp duty, personal income tax for expatriates, foreign income tax (2024 rules), Tax Court appeals, Revenue Department audits.

Transfer Pricing

Companies with related-party transactions exceeding THB 200 million per year are required to file disclosure forms and prepare full transfer pricing documentation demonstrating arm's length pricing. The Revenue Department has significantly increased transfer pricing assessments in 2024–2026.

We prepare transfer pricing documentation, conduct arm's length analysis, assist with advance pricing agreement (APA) submissions, and defend assessments before the Revenue Department and Tax Court.

What We Handle
Transfer pricing documentation and policy, arm's length analysis (comparable searches), disclosure form preparation, advance pricing agreements (APA), Revenue Department examination defence, Tax Court litigation, management fee and royalty structuring, intercompany agreement review.

BOI & Investment Promotion

BOI Application Strategy & Approval

BOI promotion under the Investment Promotion Act B.E. 2520 provides significant incentives — including CIT exemption for up to 13 years, import duty exemptions, and foreign land ownership rights — for qualifying businesses in targeted sectors such as manufacturing, technology, R&D, and services.

We advise on eligibility assessment, activity classification under the BOI's promoted categories, application strategy (Category A1 through B2), conditions negotiation, and approval processing. Applications for targeted industries — including next-generation automotive, smart electronics, biotechnology, and digital economy — receive priority treatment.

What We Handle
BOI eligibility assessment, activity and category classification, application preparation and filing, BOI conditions negotiation, project commencement approval, machine importation exemptions, raw material import duty exemptions, IBC promotion applications, IEAT Zone applications.

Post-Promotion Compliance & OSSC

BOI-promoted companies must comply with ongoing conditions — including production commencement reporting, machine importation within prescribed periods, annual performance reports, and capital investment requirements. Failure to comply risks revocation of promotion and tax benefit clawback.

BOI annual performance reports, promotion condition compliance, machine importation and disposal management, OSSC immigration processing (visa + work permit), BOI employee headcount notifications, promotion amendment applications, promotion transfer on M&A, BOI audit support and defence.

How long does it take to register a trademark in Thailand?
A straightforward trademark registration in Thailand typically takes 12 to 18 months from filing to registration. The process involves filing, examination by the Department of Intellectual Property (DIP), publication for opposition, and issuance of the registration certificate. Our IP team manages over 3,000 trademark matters annually and can advise on the fastest path for your situation.
What is the LTR Visa and who qualifies?
The Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa is a 10-year visa introduced by the Thai government for four categories of foreign nationals: Wealthy Global Citizens, Wealthy Pensioners, Work-from-Thailand Professionals, and Highly-Skilled Professionals. Benefits include a 17% flat income tax rate, exemption from the 4:1 Thai-to-foreign worker ratio, and 90-day reporting extended to annual reporting. Our immigration team handles the full application process.
Can a foreign company set up a business in Thailand?
Yes. Foreign companies can establish a presence in Thailand through several structures: a Thai Limited Company, a Branch Office, a Representative Office, or through BOI (Board of Investment) promoted activities which offer tax incentives and permit majority foreign ownership. Our corporate team advises on the optimal structure based on your business activity, investment level, and long-term goals in the Thai and ASEAN markets.
