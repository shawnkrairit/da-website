Intellectual Property

Thailand's intellectual property regime, administered by the Department of Intellectual Property (DIP) under the Ministry of Commerce, provides substantive protections across the full spectrum of IP rights. Dej-Udom & Associates has been recognised internationally for its intellectual property practice, maintaining a distinguished record in both prosecution and enforcement. Our IP division handles thousands of trademark applications annually and prosecutes infringement actions through the Central Intellectual Property and International Trade Court (IP&IT Court).

Our Services
Trademark Registration & Prosecution
Filing, prosecution, and maintenance of trademark applications before the DIP. Opposition and cancellation proceedings. Madrid Protocol designations.
Patent & Design Protection
Invention patents, petty patents, and industrial design registrations. Patent prosecution, office action responses, and annuity management.
Copyright Protection
Copyright registration and recordation at the DIP Copyright Office. Software licensing and digital content protection.
Trade Secret & Confidential Information
Trade secret protection programmes, NDA drafting and enforcement, employee invention policies.
IP Enforcement & Litigation
Civil and criminal enforcement through the IP&IT Court. Customs recordation and border measures. Raid actions coordinated with ECD and DSI.
IP Licensing & Commercialisation
Licence agreements, technology transfer agreements, franchise agreements, and royalty structuring.
Frequently Asked Questions
How long does trademark registration take in Thailand?
A straightforward registration typically takes 12–18 months. The trademark is valid for 10 years from filing and renewable indefinitely.
Can a foreign company own a trademark in Thailand?
Yes. Foreign entities may own Thai trademarks directly. The applicant must appoint a registered Thai agent. No local entity is required.
What protection does a patent provide?
An invention patent grants 20-year exclusive rights. A petty patent provides 6 years (extendable to 10). An industrial design patent provides 10 years.
How do I enforce my trademark against counterfeit goods?
Options include criminal complaints through the ECD or DSI, civil actions before the IP&IT Court, and border measures through Thai Customs recordation.
Is copyright registration required?
No. Copyright arises automatically. However, DIP recordation is strongly recommended as prima facie evidence of ownership.
Does Thailand participate in international IP treaties?
Yes. Thailand is a member of WIPO, the Paris Convention, PCT, Madrid Protocol, Berne Convention, and TRIPS under the WTO.

Immigration & Work Permits

Thailand's immigration framework, principally governed by the Immigration Act B.E. 2522 (1979) and the Working of Aliens Act B.E. 2551 (2008), imposes layered requirements upon foreign nationals seeking to live and work in the Kingdom. Dej-Udom & Associates maintains a dedicated immigration department staffed by practitioners who manage hundreds of visa and work permit applications annually. We serve as the interface between our clients and the relevant Thai authorities — including the Office of the Immigration Bureau, the Department of Employment, and the One-Stop Service Centre (OSSC) for BOI-promoted companies.

Our Services
Non-Immigrant B Visa
Applications, extensions, and annual renewals for business-purpose entry and stay. Coordination with Thai consulates abroad and Immigration Division 1.
Work Permit Applications
TT.1 new applications, TT.3 renewals, and TT.5 amendments through the Department of Employment and e-WP online system.
BOI & OSSC Processing
Expedited immigration processing for Board of Investment promoted companies through the One-Stop Service Centre at Chamchuri Square.
Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa
Qualification assessment and application support for the 10-year LTR visa under the four qualifying categories.
Destination Thailand Visa (DTV)
Applications for digital nomads and remote workers under the DTV programme introduced in 2024.
90-Day Reporting & Re-Entry Permits
Ongoing compliance management including TM.47 notifications, single and multiple re-entry permits, and address change notifications.
Frequently Asked Questions
What documents are required for a Thai work permit application?
A work permit application (Form WP.1 or TT.1) requires the applicant's passport with valid Non-Immigrant visa, educational credentials authenticated by the Thai Embassy, medical certificate from a licensed Thai hospital, employer's corporate documents (company registration, shareholder list, VAT registration, PND.1 withholding tax filings, Social Security Fund registration), and photographs.
How long does it take to obtain a Non-Immigrant B visa and work permit?
The Non-B visa is typically issued within 3–5 working days at a Thai consulate abroad. The work permit application takes approximately 7–10 working days. For BOI-promoted companies utilising the OSSC, the combined process can be completed within 3–5 working days. Total timeline is generally 4–8 weeks.
Can I change employers without leaving Thailand?
Yes. A job change requires cancellation of the existing work permit by the former employer, application for a new work permit by the new employer, and transfer of the visa endorsement. This must be coordinated carefully to avoid gaps in legal status. The process typically takes 5–10 working days.
What is the foreign-to-Thai employee ratio requirement?
The employer must employ four Thai nationals for each foreign work permit holder and maintain a minimum registered capital of THB 2 million per foreign employee. BOI-promoted companies are exempt from these ratios.
What happens to my visa if my work permit is cancelled?
The foreign national's permission to stay is typically revoked, and the individual is granted a 7-day period (up to 30 days at discretion) to depart the Kingdom or secure alternative legal status.
Does Thailand offer a digital nomad visa?
Yes. The Destination Thailand Visa (DTV), introduced on 1 June 2024, permits a stay of up to 180 days (extendable once) for remote workers and digital nomads.

Corporate & M&A

Foreign investment in Thailand operates within a regulatory framework shaped by the Civil and Commercial Code, the Foreign Business Act B.E. 2542, and sector-specific legislation. Dej-Udom & Associates has guided foreign investors through Thai corporate law since 1986 — from initial market entry structuring through ongoing governance to complex M&A transactions. We place particular emphasis on compliance with the Foreign Business Act, whose enforcement has intensified significantly in 2024–2026.

Foreign Business Licence (FBL)
Applications under Lists 2 and 3 of the Foreign Business Act. Strategic analysis including Treaty of Amity route.
Mergers & Acquisitions
Buy-side and sell-side advisory, legal due diligence, share and asset purchase agreements.
Joint Ventures

Board governance advisory, statutory compliance, anti-corruption policies, and whistleblower frameworks.

Group restructuring, demergers, amalgamations, dissolution and liquidation proceedings.
Frequently Asked Questions
Can a foreigner own 100% of a Thai company?
It depends on the activity. 100% ownership is achievable through BOI promotion, a Foreign Business Licence, the US-Thailand Treaty of Amity, or unrestricted activities.
What is the minimum capital requirement?
No statutory minimum for a standard company. THB 2 million per foreign work permit holder is required. BOI companies must meet prescribed thresholds.
What are the consequences of nominee shareholders?
Nominee arrangements are illegal. In 2024–2025, authorities targeted 46,000+ companies with hundreds of prosecutions and dissolution orders.
How long does incorporation take?
Approximately 2–4 weeks. From 2026, fully digitised through DBD Biz Regist. FBL adds 3–6 months; BOI adds 60–90 days.
What ongoing compliance obligations exist?
Audited financial statements, CIT returns, withholding tax returns, AGMs, DBD filings, VAT registration, and Social Security contributions.

How long does commercial litigation take?
First instance: 1–3 years. Appeal: 1–2 years additional. Supreme Court: 2–3 years additional. IP&IT and Labour Courts: 8–18 months.
Can foreign arbitral awards be enforced?
Yes. Thailand is a New York Convention signatory. Enforcement must be filed within three years.
What are the advantages of arbitration?
English-language proceedings, party-selected arbitrators, finality, confidentiality, and cross-border enforcement via the New York Convention.
What remedies are available?
Monetary damages, specific performance, injunctions, declaratory relief, rescission, and asset attachment.
Must proceedings be in Thai?
Yes. All filings, evidence, and arguments in Thai. Foreign documents require certified translation.

Employment & Labour Law

Thai employment law is principally governed by the Labour Protection Act B.E. 2541 (1998), as amended in 2019, together with the Labour Relations Act, Social Security Act, and related legislation. Dej-Udom & Associates advises employers across all facets — from structuring employment relationships and drafting contracts through compliance management to termination and post-employment restrictions. Thai employment law is fundamentally protective of employees, and the Labour Court consistently demonstrates a pro-employee orientation.

Our Services
Employment Contracts & Policies
Employment contracts, work rules, handbooks, and HR policy manuals. Bilingual Thai-English documentation.
Termination & Severance
Lawful termination procedures, statutory severance calculation, and termination for cause under Section 119.
Labour Disputes & Litigation
Representation before the Labour Court in wrongful termination and wage disputes.
Social Security & Provident Fund
SSF registration, workmen's compensation, and provident fund administration.
Non-Compete & Confidentiality
Drafting and enforcement of non-competition agreements under Thai Supreme Court jurisprudence.
Workforce Restructuring
Redundancy programmes, mass termination, business transfers, and Section 75 notifications.
Frequently Asked Questions
What severance pay is required upon termination?
30 days' wages (120 days–1 year); 90 days' (1–3 years); 180 days' (3–6 years); 240 days' (6–10 years); 300 days' (10–20 years); 400 days' (20+ years). Plus special severance of 15 days/year for 6+ years of service.
Can employers terminate without cause?
Yes, but with advance notice and full statutory severance. The Labour Court may order reinstatement if termination is found unfair.
Are non-compete agreements enforceable?
Yes, if reasonable in scope, duration (1–2 years), and geography. Thai courts do not narrow overbroad clauses — they strike them down entirely.
What are statutory leave entitlements?
Annual leave (6 days minimum), sick leave (30 paid days), personal leave (3 paid days), maternity (98 days), plus 13 traditional holidays and weekly rest.
What happens to employees in a business sale?
Under Section 13, the buyer assumes all obligations. The transfer cannot diminish rights. Employees refusing transfer receive severance from the seller.

Data Privacy & PDPA

Thailand's PDPA B.E. 2562 (2019), in full force since 1 June 2022, establishes obligations for data controllers and processors, grants data subjects enforceable rights, and imposes fines up to THB 5 million, criminal penalties, and civil liability. The PDPC has progressively issued subordinate regulations on consent, data breach notification, DPO appointment, and cross-border transfers. Dej-Udom & Associates provides end-to-end PDPA advisory — from gap analysis through DPO support and breach response.

Our Services
PDPA Compliance Programme Design
Gap analysis, compliance roadmaps, privacy policies, ROPA, and lawful basis assessments.
Data Protection Officer (DPO) Services
DPO appointment advisory, outsourced DPO services, and PDPC interface management.
Data Breach Response
Incident response planning, PDPC notification within 72 hours, and remediation programmes.
Cross-Border Data Transfer
Transfer mechanism assessment including adequacy, BCRs, and standard contractual clauses.
Privacy Impact Assessment
DPIAs for high-risk processing, legitimate interest assessments, and balancing tests.
PDPA Training & Awareness
Organisation-wide training, department workshops, and board-level governance briefings.
Frequently Asked Questions
Does the PDPA apply to foreign companies?
Yes. The PDPA applies to any organisation collecting or processing personal data of individuals in Thailand, regardless of where the organisation is established.
When must a DPO be appointed?
Since December 2023, required where core activities involve large-scale processing requiring regular monitoring, or large-scale processing of sensitive data.
What are the penalties for non-compliance?
Administrative fines up to THB 5 million; criminal penalties up to 1 year/THB 1 million; civil liability with punitive damages up to 2x actual damages. Personal officer liability applies.
How does the PDPA differ from the GDPR?
Key differences: broader lawful bases, no formal data portability, different cross-border rules, criminal penalties, and still-developing subordinate regulations.
What should be done in a data breach?
Assess severity, notify PDPC within 72 hours, notify affected data subjects if high risk, and document the breach and remedial actions.

Taxation

Thailand's tax system imposes CIT at 20%, personal income tax up to 35%, VAT at 7%, specific business tax, and stamp duty. A fundamental shift occurred on 1 January 2024 restructuring foreign-sourced income taxation — Thai tax residents who bring foreign income into Thailand are now assessable regardless of when earned. Dej-Udom & Associates provides strategic tax advisory integrating domestic law with 60+ double taxation agreements.

Our Services
Corporate Tax Planning
Tax-efficient structuring, profit repatriation, BOI incentives, and PE risk management.
Transfer Pricing
Documentation, arm's length analysis, advance pricing agreements, and defence of assessments.
International Tax & Treaty Planning
Cross-border structuring with 60+ DTAs, withholding tax optimisation, and holding structures.
Tax Disputes & Controversy
Assessment appeals, Tax Court litigation, and negotiated settlements.
VAT & Indirect Taxes
VAT compliance, specific business tax, customs duty, and excise matters.
Personal Tax & Expatriate Tax
Residency analysis, 2024 foreign income rules, and expatriate compensation structuring.
Frequently Asked Questions
What is the corporate income tax rate?
Standard 20%. SMEs: 0% on first THB 300,000, 15% thereafter. BOI companies: up to 13 years' exemption.
How are foreign companies taxed?
Branches/PEs: 20% on Thai-sourced income plus 10% profit remittance tax. Subsidiaries: worldwide income with DTA-reduced withholding on dividends.
What are transfer pricing requirements?
Companies with related-party transactions exceeding THB 200M/year must file disclosure forms and prepare full documentation.
How has foreign income taxation changed?
Since 1 January 2024, Thai tax residents are assessable on remitted foreign income regardless of when earned.
Can DTAs reduce withholding tax?
Yes. 60+ DTAs typically reduce dividends to 5–10%, interest to 10–15%, and royalties to 5–15%.

Real Estate & Property

Thailand's property law, anchored by the Land Code B.E. 2497, Condominium Act B.E. 2522, and the Civil and Commercial Code, imposes fundamental restrictions on foreign land ownership while providing defined investment pathways. The environment has tightened in 2024–2026 — the Supreme Court's March 2025 decision invalidated "30+30+30" lease structures, and nominee enforcement targeted 46,000+ companies. Dej-Udom & Associates advises on transactions structured within Thai law.

Our Services
Condominium Acquisition
Foreign freehold within the 49% quota, Tor Tor 3 compliance, and Land Department registration.
Leasehold Structuring
Long-term leases post-2025 Supreme Court ruling, commercial leases, and renewal strategies.
Property Due Diligence
Title search, encumbrances, zoning, environmental assessments, and building permits.
Commercial Property
Office and retail leases, industrial property, and logistics facility structuring.
Construction & Development
Construction contracts, EIA compliance, permits, and project finance.
Property Disputes
Boundary disputes, lease termination, construction defects, and Land Department proceedings.
Frequently Asked Questions
Can foreigners own land in Thailand?
Generally no. Narrow exceptions: THB 40M+ investment under Section 96 bis, BOI-promoted companies, Industrial Estates, and US Treaty of Amity. Nominee structures are illegal.
Can foreigners own condominiums?
Yes. Freehold ownership if building's foreign quota is under 49% and funds are remitted from abroad (Tor Tor 3 form required).
What happened to "30+30+30" leases?
The Supreme Court's March 2025 decision confirmed renewals beyond 30 years are not enforceable against subsequent landowners.
What due diligence is needed?
Title verification, encumbrance search, zoning, building permits, environmental assessment, access roads, foreign quota check, and litigation review.
What are transfer costs?
Transfer fee (2%), specific business tax (3.3% if under 5 years) or stamp duty (0.5% if over 5 years), and withholding income tax.

Banking & Finance

Thailand's financial services framework is supervised by three regulators: the Bank of Thailand (BOT), the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), and the Office of Insurance Commission (OIC). The landscape has transformed in 2024–2026 with virtual banking licences, the BOT's Programmable Payment Sandbox, and stricter foreign exchange documentation. Dej-Udom & Associates advises on regulatory compliance, transactions, and market entry at the intersection of financial regulation, corporate law, and foreign investment.

Project & Structured Finance

Loan documentation, mortgages, pledges, Business Security Act, and enforcement.
Frequently Asked Questions
What licences are needed for fintech?
E-payment: BOT licence. Digital asset exchange: SEC licence. P2P lending: BOT sandbox. Insurtech: OIC regulation.
Can foreign banks operate in Thailand?
Through existing licensed branches (no new licences since 2008), subsidiary banks, or representative offices. Virtual banking is a new 2025 pathway.
What foreign exchange controls apply?
Foreign currency over USD 50,000 must be converted/deposited within 360 days. Outward remittance needs documentation. Inbound investment requires Tor Tor 3.
How are digital assets regulated?
Under the 2018 Emergency Decree, supervised by the SEC. All digital asset businesses need SEC licensing.
What are virtual banking regulations?
Three licences approved mid-2025. Operations expected 2026 under restricted initial phase. Branchless, technology-driven models for financial inclusion.

BOI & Investment Promotion

The BOI, under the Investment Promotion Act B.E. 2520, confers CIT exemptions up to 13 years, import duty exemptions, 100% foreign ownership, land ownership permission, and expedited OSSC processing. The current framework categorises activities into groups A1+ through A4, with enhanced incentives for EEC investments. Dej-Udom & Associates manages the full BOI lifecycle: pre-application analysis, application, approval, and post-promotion compliance.

Our Services
BOI Application Strategy
Feasibility analysis, activity classification, incentive optimisation, and business plan preparation.
BOI Application & Approval
Application preparation, review panel presentations, condition negotiation, and certificate issuance.
Post-Promotion Compliance
Annual reporting (Bor.1), CIT exemption management, import duties, and machine lists.
OSSC Immigration Services
Expedited work permits and visas through the One-Stop Service Centre.
EEC & Special Zone Incentives
Enhanced incentives for Eastern Economic Corridor and special economic zones.
BOI Audit Defence & Amendment
Audit defence, condition amendments, activity scope changes, and disputes.
Frequently Asked Questions
What are the main BOI benefits?
CIT exemption 3–13 years, 50% CIT reduction for 5 more years, import duty exemptions, 100% foreign ownership, land ownership, OSSC immigration, and forex remittance.
How long does the BOI application take?
60–90 working days for review. Six months to accept and satisfy pre-operation conditions.
What businesses qualify?
Activities across agriculture, manufacturing, technology, services, and infrastructure. Maximum incentives for digital tech, biotech, EV, aerospace, and medical devices.
Can BOI companies employ unlimited foreign workers?
Exempt from the 4:1 ratio, but positions must be approved in the application. Technology transfer plans may be required.
What if compliance fails?
Suspension or revocation triggers repayment of all tax benefits plus surcharges. Audits have intensified.

What practice areas does Dej-Udom & Associates cover?

How long does it take to register a trademark in Thailand?
A straightforward trademark registration in Thailand typically takes 12 to 18 months from filing to registration. The process involves filing, examination by the Department of Intellectual Property (DIP), publication for opposition, and issuance of the registration certificate. Our IP team manages over 3,000 trademark matters annually and can advise on the fastest path for your situation.
What is the LTR Visa and who qualifies?
The Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa is a 10-year visa introduced by the Thai government for four categories of foreign nationals: Wealthy Global Citizens, Wealthy Pensioners, Work-from-Thailand Professionals, and Highly-Skilled Professionals. Benefits include a 17% flat income tax rate, exemption from the 4:1 Thai-to-foreign worker ratio, and 90-day reporting extended to annual reporting. Our immigration team handles the full application process.
Can a foreign company set up a business in Thailand?
Yes. Foreign companies can establish a presence in Thailand through several structures: a Thai Limited Company, a Branch Office, a Representative Office, or through BOI (Board of Investment) promoted activities which offer tax incentives and permit majority foreign ownership. Our corporate team advises on the optimal structure based on your business activity, investment level, and long-term goals in the Thai and ASEAN markets.
