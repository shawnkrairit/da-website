BOI Investment Promotion in 2026: Updated Incentives and Eligible Activities

By Attorney Name (Corporate) 28 February 2026 7 min read

Background: Thailand's BOI Promotion Framework

Thailand's Board of Investment (BOI) has long served as the primary government agency responsible for encouraging domestic and foreign investment in key sectors of the Thai economy. Through a system of tax and non-tax incentives, the BOI aims to attract capital, technology, and expertise into industries that align with the country's economic development priorities. The BOI's promotion framework is periodically updated to reflect shifting policy goals, and 2026 brings a number of noteworthy changes that companies considering investment in Thailand should understand.

The BOI's incentive structure is organised around a classification of eligible activities, grouped by their strategic importance to the national economy. Activities designated as having the highest value receive the most generous tax incentives, while those of lesser strategic priority receive correspondingly more modest benefits. This tiered approach allows the BOI to direct investment toward sectors that the government has identified as critical for long-term economic growth and competitiveness.

Eligible Activities and Priority Sectors

The BOI's revised promotion list for 2026 continues to prioritise industries aligned with Thailand's economic transformation agenda. Key sectors receiving the highest level of incentives include advanced automotive manufacturing (particularly electric vehicles and components), electronics and smart devices, digital technology and software development, biotechnology and advanced agriculture, automation and robotics, and medical and wellness tourism infrastructure. Companies operating in these sectors may qualify for corporate income tax exemptions of up to eight years, with no cap on the exempted amount for activities classified in the top tier.

In addition to these flagship sectors, the BOI continues to promote a broad range of manufacturing, services, and infrastructure activities. Notable additions to the 2026 list include expanded categories for data centre operations, semiconductor design and fabrication, and activities related to the circular economy and sustainable manufacturing. The BOI has also introduced enhanced incentives for companies that commit to significant research and development expenditure or technology transfer activities within Thailand.

Tax and Non-Tax Benefits

BOI-promoted companies enjoy a range of tax incentives depending on the classification of their approved activity. At the highest tier, these include full corporate income tax exemptions for up to eight years, with possible extensions for projects located in designated economic zones or special development areas. Additional benefits may include exemptions or reductions on import duties for machinery and raw materials, and permission to own land for promoted activities.

Non-tax benefits are equally significant for foreign investors. BOI-promoted companies are entitled to bring in foreign nationals to work in skilled and management positions, with streamlined work permit and visa processing through the BOI's One-Start One-Stop Investment Center. Promoted companies also benefit from the right to remit foreign currency abroad, which provides important operational flexibility for multinational groups with cross-border treasury requirements.

Application Process

The BOI application process begins with the submission of an investment project proposal, which must detail the nature of the activity, the proposed investment amount, projected employment, and the technology or expertise to be deployed. The BOI evaluates each application against its published criteria and may request additional information or modifications before granting approval. Processing times typically range from 40 to 90 working days, depending on the complexity of the project and the completeness of the initial submission.

Once approved, the promoted company must comply with certain conditions, including minimum investment thresholds, employment of Thai nationals, and timely commencement of operations. Failure to meet these conditions can result in revocation of the promotional privileges. The BOI conducts periodic compliance reviews, and promoted companies are required to submit annual operating reports.
