Thailand's LTR Visa: A Practical Guide for Qualifying Applicants in 2026

By Prof. Dej-Udom Krairit 12 March 2026 8 min read
Listen to this article
8 min audio summary narrated by the author

Introduction to the LTR Visa

Thailand's Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa was introduced as part of the government's strategy to attract high-potential individuals and stimulate economic growth. The programme offers a ten-year visa with a range of privileges designed to make Thailand a more competitive destination for wealthy individuals, retirees, remote workers, and highly skilled professionals. As of 2026, the LTR Visa remains one of the most attractive residency options in Southeast Asia, offering a combination of extended stay rights, work authorisation, and significant tax benefits that few competing programmes can match.

Administered by the Board of Investment (BOI), the LTR Visa programme reflects Thailand's broader push to position itself as a regional hub for talent and investment. Unlike traditional visa categories, the LTR Visa is explicitly designed for individuals who can contribute to the Thai economy through spending, investment, or the transfer of specialised skills and knowledge.

Eligibility Categories

The LTR Visa programme is open to four distinct categories of applicants, each with its own qualification criteria. Understanding which category applies is the essential first step in any application.

Wealthy Global Citizens

This category targets high-net-worth individuals with a minimum personal net worth of USD 1 million and a minimum annual income of USD 80,000 in the two years preceding the application. Applicants must also hold investments in Thai government bonds, foreign direct investment, or Thai property of at least USD 500,000.

Wealthy Pensioners

Designed for retirees aged 50 and above, this category requires a minimum annual pension or personal income of USD 80,000. Applicants with income between USD 40,000 and USD 80,000 may still qualify provided they invest at least USD 250,000 in Thai government bonds or property.

Work-from-Thailand Professionals

This category is aimed at remote workers employed by established foreign companies. Applicants must demonstrate a minimum personal income of USD 80,000 per year over the preceding two years and be employed by a company with revenues of at least USD 150 million in the previous three years, or by a publicly listed company.

Highly Skilled Professionals

Targeting specialists in targeted industries such as automotive, electronics, smart technology, automation, digital, and biotechnology, this category requires a minimum personal income of USD 80,000 per year. Applicants with income between USD 40,000 and USD 80,000 may qualify if they hold a master's degree or above, or possess intellectual property or specialised expertise in the relevant field.

Application Process Overview

The LTR Visa application is submitted online through the BOI's dedicated LTR portal. The process begins with a pre-qualification check, where applicants provide documentation of their eligibility under the relevant category. Required documents typically include proof of income or net worth, employment contracts or pension statements, investment certificates, and health insurance coverage with a minimum of USD 50,000 in coverage. Processing times have generally ranged from 20 to 60 business days, though this can vary depending on the completeness of the submission and the applicant's category.

Upon approval, the applicant receives an endorsement letter from the BOI, which is then used to apply for the LTR Visa stamp at a Thai embassy or consulate, or at the Bangkok Immigration Bureau for those already in Thailand. The visa is initially granted for five years and is renewable for an additional five-year term.

Tax Benefits

One of the most significant advantages of the LTR Visa is its tax treatment. Holders in the "Highly Skilled Professionals" and "Work-from-Thailand Professionals" categories are eligible for a flat personal income tax rate of 17% on income earned from employment in Thailand. This represents a substantial reduction from the standard progressive rates, which can reach up to 35%. Additionally, LTR Visa holders are exempt from the requirement to report for 90-day check-ins at immigration, a significant administrative convenience.

It is important to note that the 17% flat rate applies specifically to Thai-sourced employment income. Other income categories may still be subject to standard Thai tax rules, and careful planning is advisable to ensure that the full range of benefits is properly utilised.
