# Intellectual Property

Our IP Department handles every stage of intellectual property protection in Thailand and across the region — from initial filing and registration through to enforcement, licensing, and commercialisation. We act for multinational brand owners, technology companies, pharmaceutical groups, and FMCG businesses managing portfolios that span multiple jurisdictions and asset classes.

The department covers trademarks, patents, copyrights, and related rights, with a dedicated overseas filing desk coordinating protection across ASEAN and internationally through our membership in INTA, APAA, and the International Lawyers Network.

---

## Trademark

We advise on the full lifecycle of trademark protection under Thai law: clearance searches, filing, prosecution, opposition and cancellation proceedings, renewals, assignments, and licensing. Our trademark practice extends to service marks, house marks, certification marks, and collective marks.

Where brand owners face infringement, we coordinate enforcement through the Department of Intellectual Property, Thai Customs, and the Central Intellectual Property and International Trade Court. This includes border measures, customs seizures, and civil litigation to secure injunctive relief and damages.

For companies entering the Thai market, we provide clearance opinions and filing strategies that account for the classification requirements and examination practices of the Thai DIP. For global brands already operating here, we manage renewal portfolios, recordals, and watch services to maintain and defend existing registrations.

Our trademark team regularly handles passing off matters and IP commercialisation work, including franchise agreements, distribution and licensing structures, and co-branding arrangements.

**What we handle:**
Registration and prosecution, opposition and cancellation, enforcement and anti-counterfeiting, customs recordals, licensing and assignments, passing off, IP commercialisation, portfolio management and renewals.

## Patent

We advise on patent protection for inventions, industrial designs, and petty patents (utility models) under the Thai Patent Act.

For invention patents, we handle drafting and filing of applications, prosecution through the Department of Intellectual Property, responses to official actions and examiner objections, and maintenance of granted patents through the 20-year term. Thailand offers several mechanisms to accelerate substantive examination — including the ASEAN Patent Examination Co-operation (ASPEC) programme, the Patent Prosecution Highway (PPH) with the Japan Patent Office, and the Target Patent Fast-Track for applications in medical science, public health, future food, and green innovation. We advise clients on which route best fits their filing strategy and commercial timeline.

For PCT national phase applications, we manage entry into the Thai national phase within the prescribed deadlines, coordinate Thai-language translation of specifications and claims, and prosecute applications through examination. Where international search or examination reports are available from IP5 offices, we use these to support a more efficient examination process in Thailand.

Design patents protect the ornamental or aesthetic aspects of industrial products, with a 10-year term. Petty patents cover functional innovations that may not meet the inventive step threshold for a full invention patent, with a 6-year initial term extendable to 10 years. We handle both from filing through to grant and renewal.

For all patent types, we manage annuity payments, recordals of ownership changes and assignments, and licence registrations with the DIP.

**What we handle:**
Invention patents, design patents, petty patents, PCT national phase entry, patent prosecution and official action responses, accelerated examination (ASPEC, PPH, Target Patent), annuity management, assignments and licence recordals, patent searches and freedom-to-operate opinions.

## Overseas IP

For clients who need IP protection beyond Thailand, our overseas filing desk coordinates trademark and patent applications across ASEAN and internationally.

We work as the instructing firm, liaising with trusted local counsel in each jurisdiction while maintaining a single point of contact for the client. **What we handle:**
Multi-jurisdiction trademark and patent filings across ASEAN, international filing coordination through ILN and INTA networks, Madrid Protocol designations, inbound PCT national phase management, consolidated portfolio reporting.

# Immigration & Relocation

Our Immigration and Relocation Department advises on all aspects of employing foreign nationals in Thailand — from obtaining work permits and visas before arrival through to long-term residency planning. Since March 2023, the firm has been appointed as a BOI Certified Agency for the Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa, one of a limited number of firms authorised to submit applications directly to the Board of Investment.

We act for multinational employers, regional headquarters, BOI-promoted companies, SMEs, and individual applicants. The department handles both high-volume corporate accounts managing dozens of expatriate employees and individual matters requiring close attention to specific circumstances.

---

## Work Permits

We handle new work permit applications, renewals, amendments, and cancellations under both the BOI single-window system and the standard process through the Department of Employment.

For BOI-promoted companies, applications are processed through the Thailand Investment and Expat Services Center (TIESC) using the online single-window system. This covers new work permits (both long-term and short-term), renewals, temporary 30-day permits, and cancellations. We manage the pre-approval process, candidate nomination, and accompany employees to TIESC to obtain the Digital Work Permit (DWP).

For non-BOI companies, we handle applications through the TIESC or Employment Department directly. This includes the pre-approval (WP3) route, the standard Bor Thor 25 process, renewals, and amendments for changes of workplace or personal particulars. Where urgent placement is required, we can arrange urgent work permit applications with a one-day processing timeline.

We also coordinate the MedHelp service — arranging a medical team to conduct health check-ups at the expatriate's residence or workplace and collecting the medical certificate required for work permit applications.

**What we handle:**
BOI and non-BOI work permit applications, renewals and amendments, Digital Work Permits (DWP), temporary and urgent work permits, work permit cancellations, change of employer, medical certificate coordination.

---

## Visas

We advise on the full range of visa categories available to foreign nationals working or residing in Thailand.

For most business visa matters, this means the Non-Immigrant B visa — processed simultaneously with the work permit application for employees of BOI-promoted companies, or through the Immigration Bureau for non-BOI companies. We handle long-term extensions (over 6 months), short-term extensions, and renewals, including accompanying employees to the relevant office for visa stamps.

Beyond business visas, we assist with dependent visas for family members, retirement visas, the Smart Visa for qualified professionals in targeted industries, and permanent residency applications. We also handle the administrative requirements that accompany any visa: re-entry permits to preserve visa validity during travel, 90-day reporting for stays exceeding 90 days, transfer of visa endorsements to new passports, and corrections to visa endorsements.

For employees who need a visa before arriving in Thailand, we prepare the sponsor letter and supporting documentation for Non-Immigrant B or O visa applications at Thai embassies and consulates overseas.

**What we handle:**
Non-Immigrant B and O visas, visa extensions and renewals, dependent visas, retirement visas, Smart Visa, permanent residency, re-entry permits, 90-day reporting, transfer of endorsement (TOE), overseas visa support letters.

## Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa

The LTR Visa is a 10-year visa programme administered by the Board of Investment, designed for high-potential individuals who want to live and work in Thailand long-term. The visa is divided into two terms of 5 years each and is available to four categories of applicants: wealthy global citizens, wealthy pensioners, work-from-Thailand professionals, and highly-skilled professionals.

As a BOI Certified Agency, we manage the entire LTR application process — from the initial eligibility assessment and pre-screening through document preparation, submission to the LTR Unit, and obtaining the visa stamp and Digital Work Permit. We also handle renewals for the second 5-year term and dependent applications.

The LTR Visa carries significant privileges that make it one of the most attractive long-stay options in Thailand: a 10-year renewable visa, exemption from the 4:1 Thai-to-foreigner employment ratio, airport fast-track services, extension of the 90-day report to an annual report, a digital work permit, reduced income tax of 17% for highly-skilled professionals, and tax exemption on overseas income.

We provide a free initial consultation and pre-screening to assess eligibility before any engagement, so applicants understand their position before committing to the process.

**What we handle:**
LTR Visa eligibility assessment and pre-screening, application preparation and filing, coordination with the BOI LTR Unit, visa stamp and Digital Work Permit issuance, dependent applications, LTR Visa renewals, annual reporting.

# Corporate & Investment

Our Corporate Department advises multinational corporations, joint ventures, SMEs, and entrepreneurs on establishing and operating businesses in Thailand. We handle the legal, regulatory, and administrative requirements across the full business lifecycle — from initial market entry through to ongoing operations, expansion, and where necessary, restructuring or exit.

The department works closely with our taxation, immigration, and IP teams, reflecting the reality that most corporate matters in Thailand involve overlapping regulatory requirements. A single investment project, for example, may require entity formation, BOI promotion, work permits for foreign management, and trademark registration — all coordinated under one engagement.

---

## Market Entry & Formation

We advise on the most appropriate legal structure for entering the Thai market, taking into account the Foreign Business Act, BOI incentives, and the client's commercial objectives.

This includes incorporation of Thai limited companies and public companies, registration of branch offices and representative offices, structuring of joint ventures with Thai partners, and applications for Foreign Business Licences (FBL) where the proposed business activities are restricted under the Foreign Business Act. We also advise on factory licences, import and export permits, and other business licences required for specific industries.

For companies eligible for investment promotion, we prepare and submit applications to the Board of Investment (BOI) and manage the post-approval process including obtaining the BOI promotion certificate, applying for tax and non-tax incentives, and meeting operational conditions. We similarly handle applications for IEAT (Industrial Estate Authority of Thailand) privileges where the business will operate in an industrial estate or zone.

**What we handle:**
Company incorporation (Thai limited company, public company, branch office, representative office), joint venture structuring, Foreign Business Licence (FBL) applications, BOI promotion applications, IEAT privileges, factory licences, import/export permits, business licensing.

We also advise on post-completion matters including integration of acquired entities, regulatory notifications, and transfer of licences and permits.

For cross-border transactions, we coordinate with the client's international counsel and provide Thai-law opinions on matters including foreign ownership restrictions, exchange control requirements, and tax implications of the proposed structure.

# Litigation & Dispute Resolution

Our Litigation Department handles complex, high-value disputes across Thailand's civil, criminal, and specialised courts — as well as arbitration and mediation proceedings.

The department represents clients from the Courts of First Instance through the Courts of Appeal to the Supreme Court, with particular depth in commercial disputes, employment and labour matters, intellectual property and international trade cases, and tax disputes. We also represent clients in criminal matters, including white-collar crime, fraud, and regulatory offences.

For disputes involving international parties, we regularly handle matters before the Thai Arbitration Institute and advise on enforcement of foreign arbitral awards under the New York Convention. We also assist with mediation proceedings where early resolution is in the client's commercial interest.

Our approach is to give clients a clear assessment of the merits, likely timeline, and costs at the outset — and to pursue resolution through the most practical and cost-effective route available. That may mean litigation where the facts and law are strongly in our favour, arbitration where the contract requires it, or negotiated settlement where the commercial relationship warrants it.

**What we handle:**
Civil and commercial litigation, criminal defence and prosecution, labour and employment disputes, IP and international trade court proceedings, tax court matters, bankruptcy and restructuring proceedings, domestic and international arbitration, mediation, enforcement of foreign judgments and arbitral awards.

# Taxation

We advise on all matters relating to taxation in Thailand, covering both the planning and compliance aspects that businesses and individuals encounter when operating in or investing into the country.

On the corporate side, this includes structuring advice on corporate income tax, value added tax (VAT), customs and excise duties, specific business tax, withholding tax, and transfer pricing. We assist with tax planning for inbound investments, advise on the application of Thailand's double tax treaties (currently in force with 61 countries), and represent clients in dealings with the Revenue Department including tax audits, assessments, and appeals.

For individual clients, we advise on personal income tax obligations for expatriates, tax residency issues, and the tax implications of various visa and residency categories — including the reduced 17% flat rate available to highly-skilled professionals under the LTR Visa programme.

We also advise on customs and excise matters, including classification disputes, duty drawback applications, and free zone arrangements. Our tax practice works in close coordination with the corporate and immigration departments, particularly on BOI-promoted investments where tax incentives are a central part of the investment structure.

**What we handle:**
Corporate income tax planning and compliance, VAT advisory, customs and excise, withholding tax, transfer pricing, double tax treaty application, tax audit defence and appeals, personal income tax for expatriates, BOI tax incentives, tax structuring for cross-border investments.

# Notarial Services

In Thailand, notarial functions are performed by licensed Notarial Services Attorneys, regulated under the Lawyers Act B.E. 2528 and overseen by the Lawyers Council of Thailand. Our firm maintains licensed Notarial Services Attorneys who provide a full range of notarial services for both domestic and international clients.

This is particularly relevant for foreign companies and individuals who require notarised or certified documents for use in Thailand or in other jurisdictions — including powers of attorney for foreign proceedings, certified copies of corporate documents, signature verification, and sworn statements.

**What we handle:**
Certification of document copies, signature verification and authentication, recording of statements under oath, administration of oaths, ID verification and certification, powers of attorney for foreign jurisdictions.
